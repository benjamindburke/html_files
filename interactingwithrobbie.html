How did Robbie become my pet?

Robbie Rotten finished his run on the show Lazy Town, and he was let go from the studio. Tired and poor from his villainous work, he wandered the streets for weeks before I found him in the area between Northside Terrace, Race Hall and Myers Hall late one night. I saw that he was cold and hungry, so I took him in. As a person with free will, he decided to stay after he had fully recovered. He's a little weird, so he prefers being pet over being talked to.

States and stimuli

He can occupy several states that are decided by his hunger and happiness, and random chance. Hunger and happiness decrease 1 per second. Pressing the pet button adds 25 to happiness, and pressing feed adds 50 to hunger.

State Stimuli
Calm Caused when hunger is between 20 and 79 and happiness is betweeen 35 and 79.
Happy Caused when hunger and happiness are both between 80 and 100.
Angry Caused when hunger is greater than or equal to 20 and happiness is less than 20.
Lonely Caused when hunger is greater than or equal to 20 and happiness is between 20 and 34<./td>
Hungry Caused when hunger is less and 20 and not 0.
Dead Caused when hunger reaches 0. In this state, pet and feed do nothing, and the internal simulate function halts.
Rampage 10% chance to change from angry, and 25% chance to change from hungry.
Sad 20% chance to change from lonely.
Sleeping 5% chance to change from happy and 10% chance to change from calm.

From one programmer to another

Adding to this program is really simple. Taking it for yourself is as simple as changing the names of the .jpeg pictures and the states list. That is, if you plan on keeping the hunger and happiness bars. I intentionally made it so these were external numbers, so I ran into the problem of being able to change them outside of pressing the buttons. To address this, I made the textareas completely ignore key presses.

Furthermore, adapting it for your own use is as simple as chaning the variables and the math within the function simulate(). Making functions based on what you want the buttons to do is as simple as deleting pet() and feed() and replacing them with your own functions. As long as they're placed here and attached to the correct buttons using ID's, you're good to go.
